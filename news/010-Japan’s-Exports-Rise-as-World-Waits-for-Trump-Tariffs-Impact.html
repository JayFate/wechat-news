Japan’s Exports Rise as World Waits for Trump Tariffs Impact

Japan’s exports rose for a third month in December on the back of a weaker yen, as businesses waited for further clarity on likely US trade policy in President Donald Trump’s second term.

Exports measured in value gained 2.8% from a year ago led by chip-making machinery and semiconductor parts, the Ministry of Finance reported Thursday. That beat the consensus estimate of a 2.4% increase. Imports increased 1.8%, led by calculation machinery and related goods.
